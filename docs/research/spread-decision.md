# Eval-platform spread — decision tails

## Recommendation

Conditionally back the founder's Braintrust lean for a Claude-first, code-first team — but condition it explicitly on the cost model, not just product fit. Braintrust is the strongest product on the axes that matter (immutable Eval()/experiment harness mapping 1:1 to "workflow version = eval_run with a baseline", first-class Claude tracing, Brainstore search, configurable online LLM-judge scoring 1-10% high-volume / 50-100% critical). The decisive condition is COST, not seats: Braintrust meters processed-GB + scores + judge-tokens (Pro $249/mo, 5 GB +$3/GB, 30-day retention; Starter 1 GB +$4/GB, 14-day) and online judge scoring drives a SECOND compounding meter (judge LLM tokens) on top of ingestion — a worked ~20 GB/mo scenario lands near ~$294/mo of data cost plus a separate judge-token bill. That is still ~25-33x cheaper at ingestion than Weave's $0.10/MB (~$1,900+ in the same scenario), so among hosted Claude-first options Braintrust is cost-reasonable at mid volume but scales linearly and uncapped. Adopt it ONLY behind an OpenTelemetry-GenAI instrumentation seam so the vendor is a swappable OTLP sink. Flip to Opik (Apache-2.0, OSI-approved, full eval loop free to self-host) if open-source freedom is the value; to Langfuse (MIT core, but RBAC/audit/retention/masking are EE-gated) if free eval-loop self-host is a hard requirement; to Phoenix/AX (ELv2, not OSI) if open-standards interop plus zero-cost local start dominate. None of the eval platforms ships a true online-experiment engine, so defer a dedicated A/B/experimentation platform until real learner traffic exists.

## Two-tier architecture (eleatic-offline / production-online)

Two tiers answering different questions at different lifecycle stages, sharing one substrate (the OTel span tree). TIER 1 eleatic = local-first/CI/offline: source of truth for PRE-SHIP gating. Workflow VERSION = eval_run with baseline; each generated concept page = eval_row (opaque output/expected/scores/metadata); pipeline emits EvalTrace{spans}; eleatic's pure analysis module owns auc/calibratedThreshold/labelAgreement; adjudication = human review. Runs in dev inner loop + CI and BLOCKS deploy of a worse version. Confirmed at eleatic HEAD that EvalRowRecord.trace is unknown/opaque, so a lightly-normalized OTel span array drops straight in. TIER 2 production platform = live telemetry/online scoring/human-review-at-scale for POST-SHIP operation (Braintrust recommended; Opik/Langfuse/Phoenix conditionally). SEAM = OpenTelemetry: instrument ANALYSIS->SYNTHESIS ONCE as OTel GenAI spans; a multiplexing SpanProcessor fans every finished span tree to (a) the production OTLP endpoint and (b) an in-process eleatic adapter reducing the same spans to EvalTrace{spans}. Carry the workflow-version id as ONE custom root-span attribute so the same key addresses an eleatic eval_run, a Braintrust experiment, and later an experiment arm. CRITICAL refinement vs prior draft: eleatic is now load-bearing as the durable archive of record (because Braintrust automated export is Enterprise-gated and retention is 14/30 days), so it must hold ONLY the curated/sampled/gated regression corpus (adjudicated cases + the offline eval set), NOT the full production span firehose — sized that way SQLite stays within its concurrency/durability envelope; sized as a full mirror it does not. The firehose stays in the vendor or a cheap object-store mirror. eleatic decides "is B better than baseline A offline?" and gates the deploy; the production platform answers "is B behaving well on live traffic and what do judges/humans say?"

## Instrumentation & lock-in strategy

Emit OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic-convention spans (gen_ai.*) as the SINGLE primary instrumentation — not a vendor SDK (wrapAnthropic/@traceable/@weave.op, which make the vendor your span format = lock-in) and not OpenInference (different namespace coupling you to Arize/Phoenix). Use OpenLLMetry's Anthropic instrumentor or hand-set gen_ai.* on agent/tool spans; register a BatchSpanProcessor exporting OTLP to the production backend plus a second in-process exporter to the eleatic adapter. Braintrust, Langfuse, Weave, LangSmith all map gen_ai.* directly, so it is the lowest-coupling choice for a Braintrust-leaning decision; choose OpenInference ONLY if committing to Phoenix/AX. Three verified edges: (1) OTel GenAI semconv is still Development not Stable — gen_ai.prompt/completion are deprecated for a message-structured model, conventions moved to open-telemetry/semantic-conventions-genai with NO tagged releases, so pin via OTEL_SEMCONV_STABILITY_OPT_IN=gen_ai_latest_experimental and re-check the CHANGELOG at integration; (2) cost (USD) is NOT an OTel attribute (only tokens), so the eleatic adapter injects costUsd from a local pricing map — and beware cost-map drift between your local map and the vendor's backend-computed cost; (3) OTLP-in != OTLP-out (backends transcode to proprietary stores; automated export is Enterprise-gated on Braintrust) so instrumentation is portable but history is the lock-in. ADD a redaction SpanProcessor IN YOUR OWN PROCESS that masks prompt/completion content and strips PII BEFORE the OTLP export fires — this is the real, vendor-independent compliance control (vendor-side masking like Langfuse's is EE-gated; Braintrust BAA is Enterprise). Dual-emit failure modes to handle, not assume away: span-tree flattening correctness (deep multi-agent trees can be scrambled by naive flattening), cost-map drift, CI threading of the Weave/LangSmith entity/project model, and version-id hygiene across eleatic+vendor+experiment-platform (stamp ONE hashed workflow-version id at the root, never re-derive downstream). Adapter effort ~80-150 LOC / 1-2 days is a LOW-CONFIDENCE engineering estimate from the interop profile (not a vendor-doc fact); Opik needs extra because its OTLP ingest is HTTP-only and not GenAI-semconv/OpenInference-normalized, so map fields explicitly.

## Decision questions

- Is free/cheap self-host or in-VPC data residency a HARD launch requirement or a someday-nice-to-have? Hard => Opik (Apache-2.0, full eval loop free), Langfuse (MIT core, but RBAC/audit/retention/masking are EE-gated), or Phoenix OSS (ELv2); NOT Braintrust (self-host Enterprise-only). This single answer most likely flips the recommendation.
- Will traces carry PII/regulated learner data needing a BAA or a SOC2 boundary you control? Braintrust BAA + data-plane isolation are Enterprise; Langfuse self-host enforced-retention/project-RBAC/audit/masking need the EE key (so 'free self-host' is NOT compliance-grade by itself). First control either way is a redaction SpanProcessor in your own process so raw PII never leaves; Opik OSS + your own redaction is the cheapest defensible posture if you can't fund EE/Enterprise.
- What is realistic monthly trace-data GB and online-scoring sampling rate? (Now answerable, see matrix 2.4.) Braintrust meters GB + scores + judge-tokens; model the bill at 1%/10%/50% judge sampling remembering judge sampling drives TWO meters (span scores AND judge LLM tokens). ~20 GB/mo => ~$294/mo Braintrust data cost + separate judge-token bill, vs ~$1,900+ on Weave whose $0.10/MB ingestion dominates — the volume answer can swing the vendor by an order of magnitude.
- Can we commit to OTel GenAI gen_ai.* as the single primary instrumentation, accepting Development-status churn? If yes the vendor stays swappable; if the team prefers native wrapAnthropic/@traceable UX, accept deeper lock-in consciously. Decide where redaction and the one-root version-id stamp live before wiring dual-emit.
- Re-verify the March-16-2026 Braintrust pricing pre-commit: does the Topics LLM-judge credit revert to $100/mo (after Sep 1 2026) change online-scoring economics at your volume; is the per-MTok judge-token rate (LOW-CONFIDENCE this session) what you assumed; has any Enterprise-gated feature (automated export, SSO) moved down-tier?
- Does offline eval math live ONLY in eleatic or do we duplicate AUC/calibration in the vendor? Keep auc/calibratedThreshold/labelAgreement solely in eleatic; use the vendor's autoevals/judges for production sampling and its queues for live human review only.
- What is the exit plan in 12-18 months, and is eleatic-SQLite actually fit to be the archive of record? Eval/experiment history does NOT export as OTel and Braintrust automated export is Enterprise-gated, so on Starter/Pro the exit is scripted API pulls — decide now that eleatic holds the curated/gated regression corpus (NOT the full span firehose) so SQLite stays within its concurrency/durability envelope, with a backup policy for the file. Given the consolidating landscape, treat a forced migration as likely, not hypothetical.
- (Langfuse-specific) Are we resourced to run ClickHouse+Postgres+Redis+S3, and comfortable that self-host pricing is now additive to a ClickHouse plan owned by the acquirer (Jan-2026), whose incentive is to drive ClickHouse Cloud consumption? If ops capacity is thin, Langfuse Cloud sidesteps infra but re-enters usage-based metering.

## Risks

- R1 — Braintrust automated export is Enterprise-gated (operational consequence, not a footnote): a JSON/Parquet API export exists below Enterprise, but continuous automated S3 mirroring is Enterprise-only, so on the recommended Starter/Pro tiers there is NO turnkey bulk export — only scripted manual pulls. The eleatic-SQLite-as-archive-of-record mitigation is therefore LOAD-BEARING precisely because the vendor's own exit hatch is paywalled. Decide the archive strategy before committing, not after.
- R2 — Short retention as a data-loss / forensics risk: Braintrust free 14-day / Pro 30-day, Phoenix AX Free 15-day / Pro 30-day. Production traces age out before a quarterly review or an incident post-mortem unless exported or mirrored to eleatic. Short retention + Enterprise-gated export (R1) compound: without an explicit mirror, traces needed for human-review/incident forensics are simply gone.
- R3 — Judge-cost is a SECOND, compounding meter: online LLM-as-judge scoring consumes judge LLM tokens billed SEPARATELY from ingestion/span metering (Braintrust judge tokens; LangSmith/others bill judge tokens too). Aggressive online scoring adds the cost of the eval feature ITSELF on top of trace-volume cost. Sampling rate is two levers (span volume AND judge-token volume), not one. The exact Braintrust per-MTok judge rate is low-confidence this session — verify on the live pricing page.
- R4 — Dual-emit OTel fan-out has real operational failure modes the prior draft glossed: (a) span-tree flattening correctness — deep multi-agent trees can be scrambled or have sibling structure dropped by naive flattening; (b) cost-map drift — local costUsd vs vendor-computed cost diverge if pricing tables fall out of sync; (c) CI threading of the Weave/LangSmith entity/project model (traces land in the wrong project if unthreaded); (d) double-counting / version-id hygiene across eleatic + vendor + experiment platform (must agree on ONE root-stamped workflow-version id).
- R5 — Langfuse/ClickHouse conflict-of-interest: post Jan-2026 acquisition, Langfuse self-host pricing is explicitly ADDITIVE to a ClickHouse commercial plan, and the acquirer IS ClickHouse — so the OSS roadmap and self-host economics now sit with a vendor whose incentive is to drive ClickHouse Cloud consumption. Not merely a governance watch-item; a concrete lock-in/steering risk on the self-host path.
- R6 — Single-vendor concentration / consolidating landscape (bus-factor across the stack): three of seven finalists had ownership/consolidation events in ~13 months (Langfuse->ClickHouse Jan 2026, Eppo->Datadog May 2025, Weave inside W&B). The AI-observability landscape is actively consolidating, materially raising the odds of a forced migration within the 12-18 month horizon the report cites — a reason the OTel-seam reversibility is strategic, not cosmetic.
- R7 — eleatic as a load-bearing single point: the whole reversibility thesis rests on one SQLite file being the durable system of record. That carries SQLite scaling/concurrency limits at production trace volume, backup/durability of a single file, and the question of whether a local-first store is fit to be a long-term SoR. Mitigation: eleatic holds ONLY the curated/sampled/gated regression corpus, not the full production span firehose; firehose stays in the vendor or a cheap object-store mirror. Wrong sizing turns a fit-for-purpose store into a fragile bottleneck.
- R8 — PII/redaction-at-the-exporter is the actual control, and it is missing if you rely on vendor tiers: vendor-side masking (Langfuse) is EE-gated and BAA (Braintrust) is Enterprise, so a learner-facing pipeline carrying regulated data must redact/mask prompt+completion content in its OWN process before spans leave for any cloud sink. Without this exporter-level control, raw PII crosses the boundary regardless of which vendor is chosen.
- R9 — Pricing volatility and two unresolved conflicts: Braintrust restructured pricing March-16-2026 (promo/grandfathered Topics credit reverts to $100/mo after Sep-1-2026); Arize AX Pro overage is reported inconsistently across third-party sources ($50/mo+10GB vs $3/GB+100GB); the Braintrust per-MTok judge rate could not be independently confirmed. Treat any single third-party pricing figure as indicative, not contractual; re-pull vendor pages at contract time.
