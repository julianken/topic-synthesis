## 1. TL;DR recommendation

**The evidence BACKS the founder's Braintrust lean — conditionally — for a Claude-first, code-first team that wants one polished hosted surface and accepts a proprietary core behind an OTel escape hatch. But the lean is conditional on three things, and the strongest condition is a cost question the previous draft buried.** Braintrust is the strongest *product* on the axes that matter most for `topic-synthesis`: a mature immutable `Eval()`/experiment harness that maps almost 1:1 onto your "workflow VERSION = eval_run with a baseline" model; excellent (profile-rated "first-class") Claude tracing (`wrapAnthropic`, caching, server-side tool use, managed agents, streaming, OpenAI-compatible gateway, an Anthropic case study); production-scale trace search via Brainstore; and online LLM-judge scoring on live logs with configurable sampling + BTQL filters. For a small team shipping a Claude `ANALYSIS → SYNTHESIS` pipeline, that is the lowest-friction path to "live telemetry + online scoring + human review" today.

**The lean is conditional on three load-bearing facts — and the first is now a worked cost question, not a footnote:**

1. **Braintrust does NOT meter on seats — it meters on processed-GB + scores + judge-tokens, and the GB meter is a real cliff for a chatty multi-agent pipeline that captures full prompt+completion.** Starter (free) includes **1 GB/mo, +$4/GB overage, 14-day retention**; Pro is **$249/mo flat with 5 GB/mo, +$3/GB overage, 50k scores, 30-day retention** ([Braintrust plans & limits](https://www.braintrust.dev/docs/plans-and-limits); [costbench](https://costbench.com/software/ai-observability/braintrust/)). "Unlimited seats" is true but is *not* where the bill comes from. A worked estimate at your assumed volume is in **§2.4** — the headline: full-trace capture of a multi-agent pipeline can blow past the 5 GB Pro allowance quickly, and the *second* meter (judge tokens for online LLM-as-judge) compounds on top. Sampling is the lever, but it is two levers (span volume *and* judge-token volume), not one.
2. **Self-host / data-plane isolation, SAML/OIDC, BAA, SLAs, RBAC, and *automated* S3 export are ALL Enterprise-only, custom-priced** (`supported`; [Braintrust pricing](https://www.braintrust.dev/pricing)). There is no affordable self-host and no OSS core. Note the export nuance corrected on re-pull: a JSON/Parquet **API** export exists below Enterprise, but **continuous automated S3 mirroring is Enterprise-gated** — so on Starter/Pro your exit hatch is *scripted manual pulls*, not a turnkey mirror. This makes the eleatic-SQLite-as-archive-of-record decision (§6 Q7) load-bearing, not optional — see **Risk R1**.
3. **Braintrust has no online A/B assignment + statistical-significance engine** (`supported`). Its "experiments" are offline run-comparison; online operation is logging + online *scoring*, not randomized live experimentation. This is a real category gap shared by *every* eval platform here (Langfuse, Phoenix/AX, Weave, LangSmith, Opik) — none ships a true online-experiment engine. See §5.

**Decisive de-risking move (vendor-independent):** instrument the pipeline once with **OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic conventions** and treat Braintrust as a swappable OTLP sink — never as your primary span format. This keeps *instrumentation* portable (re-point an exporter, not re-instrument) even though *eval/experiment history* inside Braintrust remains the real lock-in (it does not export as OTel, and automated export is paywalled — Risk R1).

**When a different platform wins instead — now including Opik, the only OSI-approved-open-source finalist with the full eval loop free:**

- **Opik (Comet, Apache-2.0) wins if "free as in freedom" — true OSI-approved open source with the full eval loop self-hostable — is the top value.** It is the strongest open-standards pick on the licensing axis: unlike Phoenix (ELv2, source-available, *not* OSI) and unlike Langfuse's EE-gated administration features, Opik's eval loop (judge metrics, datasets, experiments, online rules/guardrails) is Apache-2.0 and free to self-host. Costs: the OSS build has **no SSO/user-management**, and its OTLP ingest is **HTTP-only and not GenAI-semconv/OpenInference-normalized**, so your eleatic adapter must map fields explicitly (§4). A direct OSS rival to Langfuse and a cleaner license story than Phoenix — it belongs in the consideration set, not just the matrix.

- **Langfuse (OSS / self-host) wins if data residency or "bring this in-house without a license fight" is a HARD requirement — but read the compliance caveat.** Verification **`refuted`** the claim that Langfuse gates LLM-as-judge, the playground, annotation queues, and prompt experiments behind a paid license: since June 2025 those are **MIT and free to self-host with no usage caps**. **However** — and this corrects an over-sell in the prior draft — **project-level RBAC, audit logs, configurable data-retention policies, server-side data masking, and SCIM still require the paid EE license key even on the MIT self-host** ([Langfuse EE license](https://langfuse.com/self-hosting/license-key); [RBAC discussion](https://github.com/orgs/langfuse/discussions/8147)). So a PII-handling self-host *without* EE has the full eval workflow but **lacks enforced retention, project-RBAC, audit trails, and built-in data masking** — exactly the controls a compliance boundary (§6 Q2) needs. "Full eval loop, free, on your own infra" is true; "compliance-grade self-host, free" is not. Also operationally heavy (mandatory ClickHouse + Postgres + Redis + S3), and **acquired by ClickHouse on Jan 16, 2026** — and self-host pricing is now explicitly *additive to a ClickHouse commercial plan*, a concrete conflict-of-interest (see **Risk R5**), not just a governance watch-item.

- **Phoenix / Arize AX (OpenInference + OTLP) wins if open-standards interop + zero-cost local start dominate AND ELv2 (source-available, not OSI) is acceptable.** Phoenix OSS self-hosts under ELv2 (you can't offer it as a competing managed service), backs onto SQLite or Postgres, and graduates to AX without re-instrumenting; first-class Claude coverage (Anthropic + Claude Agent SDK auto-instrumentation, Claude as judge). AX Free is 25k spans/mo, 1 GB, **15-day retention**; AX Pro is **$50/mo, 50k spans, 10 GB, 30-day** ([Arize pricing](https://arize.com/pricing/); [costbench](https://costbench.com/software/ai-observability/arize-phoenix/)). Soft cost: OpenInference is its *own* attribute namespace (`openinference.*`/`llm.*`), distinct from OTel GenAI `gen_ai.*` — "OTel-native" holds at the transport layer but you incur a semantic coupling toward Arize and need a translation processor to feed `gen_ai.*` backends. AX's online-eval *streaming/continuous* packaging on self-host is **medium/low confidence** in the profile — verify before relying on it.

**On "best OTel story":** the prior draft called Langfuse "the best OTel story of the field." The profiles describe Langfuse, Phoenix, **and** Weave all as OTel-native with strong stories; the interop profile calls Langfuse "closest to OTel-native" but does **not** crown a single field winner over Phoenix. I therefore downgrade this to **"among the best, arguably closest to OTel-native"** — not "best of field."

**Net:** Braintrust for product polish and Claude depth (the founder's instinct is defensible) *if the §2.4 GB+judge bill clears your budget*; **Opik** if OSI-open-source freedom of the full eval loop is the value; **Langfuse** if free-self-host of the eval loop is a hard requirement (with the EE-compliance caveat); **Phoenix/AX** if open-standards interop + zero-cost local start dominate. The instrumentation decision (OTel GenAI) matters more than the vendor decision because it is what keeps the vendor decision reversible.

## 3. The two-tier architecture

The split is clean because eleatic and the production platform answer **different questions at different lifecycle stages**, sharing one substrate: the OpenTelemetry span tree.

**Tier 1 — eleatic (local-first / dev loop / CI / offline exploration).** Source of truth for *pre-ship gating*. A workflow VERSION = an `eval_run` with a baseline; each generated concept page = an `eval_row` (opaque `output`/`expected`/`scores`/`metadata`). The pipeline emits `EvalTrace { spans }`; eleatic's pure analysis module owns the math (`auc`, `calibratedThreshold`, `labelAgreement`); human review via adjudication. No network, single SQLite file, framework-free explorer. Runs in the dev inner loop and CI to **gate** a workflow version before it ships. The verification block confirms at eleatic repo HEAD that `EvalRowRecord.trace` is `unknown`/opaque (`getRow` surfaces it verbatim, no schema validation) — so a lightly-normalized OTel span array drops straight in.

**Tier 2 — the production platform (live telemetry / online scoring / human review at scale).** Owns *post-ship operation*: persistent multi-user trace storage, online LLM-judge scoring on sampled live traffic, alerting/monitoring, production human-review queues. This is Braintrust (recommended), or Opik/Langfuse/Phoenix under §1's conditions.

**The seam is OpenTelemetry.** Instrument `ANALYSIS → SYNTHESIS` **once** as OTel GenAI spans. A multiplexing `SpanProcessor` fans every finished span tree to (a) the production OTLP endpoint, and (b) an in-process eleatic adapter that reduces the same spans into `EvalTrace { spans }`. Carry the **workflow-version id as a single custom attribute on the root span** so one version key addresses an eleatic `eval_run`, a Braintrust experiment, and (later) an experiment treatment arm.

**Conceptual mapping (holds across candidates):** eleatic `eval_run`(+baseline) ↔ production "experiment"/dataset-run; eleatic `eval_row` ↔ production log/trace row; eleatic span `metrics{promptTokens,completionTokens,costUsd,durationMs}` ↔ OTel `gen_ai.usage.*` + duration (`costUsd` injected locally — §4); eleatic adjudication ↔ production human-review queues.

**Division of labor:** eleatic decides *"is version B better than baseline A on our offline eval rows?"* and **blocks the deploy** if not. The production platform answers *"is version B behaving well on live traffic, and what do humans/judges say about real outputs?"* Keep AUC/calibration/label-agreement math in eleatic (don't duplicate in the vendor); use the vendor's queues for *live* human review and its online judge for *production* sampling.

**eleatic is now load-bearing as the durable archive of record — which surfaces operational concerns the prior draft never examined (see Risk R7).** Because Braintrust's automated export is Enterprise-gated (R1) and its retention is 14/30 days (R2), the reversibility thesis rests on eleatic's single SQLite file being the long-term system of record. That assignment carries SQLite scaling/concurrency limits at production trace volume, backup/durability of one file, and the question of whether a local-first store is fit to be a system of record at all. **Mitigation:** eleatic should hold the *curated, sampled, gated* eval corpus (the regression set + adjudicated cases), **not** the full firehose of production spans — the firehose stays in the vendor (or a cheap object-store mirror), and only what you'd actually re-run offline lands in SQLite. Sized that way, SQLite is fit for purpose; sized as "mirror every production span," it is not. Make this explicit in the data model.

## 4. Instrumentation & lock-in strategy

**Single recommended way: emit OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic-convention spans (`gen_ai.*`), not a vendor SDK and not OpenInference.** Use OpenLLMetry's Anthropic instrumentor or hand-set `gen_ai.*` attributes on your agent/tool spans (`gen_ai.operation.name` = `invoke_agent`/`chat`/`execute_tool`; `gen_ai.provider.name=anthropic`; `gen_ai.usage.input_tokens`/`output_tokens`). Register a `BatchSpanProcessor` exporting OTLP to the production backend, plus a second in-process exporter handing finished spans to the eleatic adapter.

**Why `gen_ai.*`:**
- **vs. a vendor SDK (`wrapAnthropic`/`@traceable`/`@weave.op`):** richest native experience but makes the vendor your primary span format — the lock-in to avoid. Use the vendor SDK only as a secondary enrichment path.
- **vs. OpenInference (`openinference.*`/`llm.*`):** Apache-2.0 and OTLP-portable, but a *different attribute namespace* that couples you toward Arize/Phoenix and needs a translation processor for `gen_ai.*` backends. Choose it **only if** you commit to Phoenix/AX. Braintrust, Langfuse, Weave, LangSmith all map `gen_ai.*` directly, so it is the lowest-coupling choice for a Braintrust-leaning decision.

**Three sharp edges (all verified):**
1. **OTel GenAI semconv is still `Development`, not Stable.** `gen_ai.prompt`/`gen_ai.completion` are **deprecated** for a message-structured model (`gen_ai.input.messages`/`gen_ai.output.messages`/`gen_ai.system_instructions`); span-events are migrating to log-events. The conventions **moved to a separate repo** (`open-telemetry/semantic-conventions-genai`) with **no tagged releases** — do **not** hard-pin a single version. Pin via `OTEL_SEMCONV_STABILITY_OPT_IN=gen_ai_latest_experimental` and re-check the genai repo CHANGELOG at integration.
2. **Cost (USD) is NOT an OTel attribute** — only token counts are. The eleatic adapter must inject `costUsd` locally from a pricing map. Braintrust/Langfuse/Weave compute cost backend-side from *their own* pricing tables — which introduces **cost-map drift** (your local `costUsd` and the vendor-computed cost can diverge if the two pricing tables fall out of sync). Treat the local map as the offline source of truth and reconcile periodically; do not assume the two numbers match (R4).
3. **OTLP-in ≠ OTLP-out.** Backends (Braintrust especially) transcode OTLP into a proprietary store, so you cannot bulk-export *eval/experiment history* back out as OTel, and **automated export is itself Enterprise-gated** (R1). Instrumentation is portable; **history is the lock-in.**

**PII redaction must happen at the exporter, before spans leave for any cloud sink (the actual operational control, vendor-independent).** For a learner-facing pipeline that may carry regulated data (§6 Q2), neither Langfuse's data-masking (EE-gated on self-host) nor Braintrust's BAA (Enterprise) is the right *first* line — both are vendor-side. The robust control is a **redaction `SpanProcessor` in your own process** that masks prompt/completion content and strips PII *before* the OTLP export fires, so raw learner data never crosses the boundary regardless of vendor tier. This is cheap, vendor-portable, and the only control fully under your governance. Make it a named component, not an afterthought.

**Adapter cost estimate (carry the source's hedge): the interop profile gives a *low-confidence engineering estimate* of roughly one file, ~80-150 LOC, 1-2 days — not a verified fact, and not derivable from any vendor doc.** Treat it as a planning rough-cut, not a commitment. The work: (a) flatten the OTel parent/child span tree into eleatic's flat `spans` array preserving order — **and this flattening is a correctness risk, not a formality**: a multi-agent run is a deep tree, and naive flattening can scramble parent/child order or drop sibling structure the offline analysis depends on (R4); (b) inject `costUsd` from a local pricing map; (c) map `gen_ai.usage.input_tokens`→`promptTokens`, `output_tokens`→`completionTokens`, span duration→`durationMs`, `gen_ai.operation.name`→`kind`; (d) choose a stable `rowKey` (e.g. concept-page id). For **Opik** specifically, add extra effort: its OTel ingest is **not** semconv-normalized (HTTP-only), so the dual-emit adapter must map fields explicitly rather than assume alignment.

**Dual-emit operational failure modes (the prior draft presented fan-out as low-cost; it has real edges — R4):** beyond span-tree flattening correctness and cost-map drift above, watch (a) **CI threading of the vendor entity/project model** — Weave's W&B entity/project and LangSmith's project model must be threaded through CI runs or traces land in the wrong project; (b) **double-counting / version-id hygiene across three systems** — eleatic, the vendor, and (later) the experiment platform must agree on one `workflow_version` id or a single offline win and online win can't be reconciled to one version. Make the version-id a single hashed attribute set once at the root and never re-derived downstream.

## 5. Do we need a separate online-experiment platform at launch?

**Recommendation: NO at launch. Defer it.** The eval platform suffices for v1; a dedicated experiment platform is overkill until you have a real learner base.

Online A/B needs **traffic volume to reach statistical significance**. With few launch users, causal lift is *undefined* — pure instrumentation overhead and a second non-OTel integration surface for zero signal. (This compounds the main discovery report's §6 power finding: ~1,500 learners/arm to detect a 5pp lift in aggregate, far more than any niche topic sees.)

**Be precise about the category gap:** Braintrust's "experiments" — and Langfuse's, Phoenix's, Weave's, LangSmith's, Opik's — are **offline run-comparison over datasets**, plus (in most) **online *scoring*** of live traces. *None* randomly assigns live learners to variants or computes frequentist/Bayesian significance with CUPED/sequential testing/SRM. The eval platform proves *"artifact B scores better"*; only an experiment platform proves *"shipping B causes better completion/retention/cost-per-page."*

**Gate-chain over time:** eleatic offline gate (CI) → production online scoring on live traces (Braintrust/Opik/Langfuse/Phoenix) → **experiment platform for causal lift, added only when traffic warrants.**

**When you add it, the cost-appropriate picks — stated precisely this time:**
- **GrowthBook** — MIT **open-core**: the self-hosted edition is genuinely MIT with unlimited users/flags/experiments; **enterprise directories (SSO, data pipelines, approval workflows) sit under a separate license**, so "MIT, fully free" is true for the core but not for those add-ons.
- **Statsig** — free Developer tier (2M events/mo); Pro $150/mo. **Crucial correction to the prior draft: Statsig's free/Pro tiers are event-ingestion, NOT warehouse-native. Warehouse-Native (WHN) is an Enterprise-tier capability** ([Statsig](https://www.statsig.com/comparison/growthbook-vs-statsig-enterprises)). So the prior "both warehouse-native-capable, ~$0 until traffic" gloss is wrong: GrowthBook can read your warehouse on lower tiers; **Statsig WHN requires Enterprise.** If warehouse-native on a budget matters, that points to GrowthBook, not Statsig.
- Reserve LaunchDarkly/Optimizely for existing-ecosystem cases. **Watch-item:** Eppo is now **Datadog Experiments** (acquired May 2025; standalone purchasing effectively gone).

**Coexistence is by convention, not protocol:** no OTel/span bridge to these tools. Reuse the **same workflow-version id** as the eleatic `eval_run` id *and* the feature-flag variant key *and* the exposure-event key. Derive outcome metrics (cost, latency, tokens) from `EvalTrace` span metrics into a warehouse table the experiment platform reads — cheapest with GrowthBook (warehouse-readable on lower tiers). Flag assignment also doubles as a **safe rollout / kill-switch** for whole workflow versions.

## 6. Open decision questions

(Ordered by leverage. The first three gate the vendor choice; Q3 now carries a worked answer.)

1. **Is free/cheap self-host or in-VPC data residency a HARD requirement at launch, or someday-nice-to-have?** If hard → **Opik** (Apache-2.0, full eval loop free) or **Langfuse** (MIT, but RBAC/audit/retention/masking need EE) or **Phoenix OSS** (ELv2), *not* Braintrust (self-host Enterprise-only). If someday → Braintrust cloud is fine. *This single answer most likely flips the recommendation.*

2. **Will traces carry PII / regulated learner data requiring a BAA or a SOC2 boundary you control?** On Braintrust, BAA + data-plane isolation are Enterprise. On Langfuse self-host, **enforced retention, project-RBAC, audit logs, and server-side masking require the EE key** — so "free self-host" does *not* by itself give you a compliance-grade boundary. Either way, the first control is a **redaction `SpanProcessor` in your own process** (§4) so raw PII never leaves; vendor tiers are the *second* line. If you need vendor-enforced controls and can't fund EE/Enterprise, **Opik OSS** (Apache-2.0) plus your own redaction is the cheapest defensible posture.

3. **What is the realistic monthly trace-data volume and online-scoring sampling rate? (Now answerable — see §2.4.)** Braintrust meters processed-GB + scores + judge-tokens (Starter 1 GB/14-day; Pro 5 GB/30-day, +$3/GB). Estimate GB/mo of full prompt+completion capture, then model the bill at **1%, 10%, and 50% judge sampling** — remembering judge sampling drives *two* meters (span scores *and* judge LLM tokens, R3). At ~20 GB/mo this session estimates **~$294/mo of Braintrust data cost + a separate judge-token bill**, vs **~$1,900+ on Weave** (its $0.10/MB ingestion dominates) — so the volume answer can swing the vendor by an order of magnitude.

4. **Can we commit to OTel GenAI `gen_ai.*` as the single primary instrumentation, accepting `Development` status and churn?** If yes, the vendor stays swappable. If the team prefers richest native UX via `wrapAnthropic`/`@traceable`, accept deeper lock-in consciously. Pin `OTEL_SEMCONV_STABILITY_OPT_IN`; re-check the genai repo at integration. Also decide *where redaction and the version-id stamp live* (one root attribute, §4) before wiring dual-emit.

5. **Re-verify the March-16-2026 Braintrust pricing before committing** — whether the Topics LLM-judge credit revert to $100/mo (after Sep 1, 2026) changes online-scoring economics at your volume, whether the per-MTok judge-token rate (low-confidence this session) is what you assumed, and whether any Enterprise-gated feature (automated export, SSO) has moved down-tier.

6. **Does the offline eval math live ONLY in eleatic, or do we duplicate AUC/calibration in the vendor?** Keep `auc`/`calibratedThreshold`/`labelAgreement` solely in eleatic to avoid two sources of truth; use the vendor's `autoevals`/judges for *production* sampling and its queues for *live* human review only.

7. **What is the exit plan if we drop the vendor in 12-18 months — and is eleatic-SQLite actually fit to be the archive of record?** Instrumentation re-points trivially; eval/experiment *history* does not export as OTel and **Braintrust automated export is Enterprise-gated (R1)**, so on Starter/Pro the exit hatch is scripted API pulls. Decide now that **eleatic holds the curated/gated regression corpus** (not the full span firehose, §3) so SQLite is sized within its concurrency/durability envelope, with a backup policy for the file (R7). Given the consolidating landscape (§2.3, R6), treat a forced migration as *likely within the horizon*, not hypothetical.

8. **(Langfuse-specific, if chosen) Are we resourced to operate ClickHouse + Postgres + Redis + S3, and comfortable that self-host pricing is now additive to a ClickHouse plan owned by the acquirer?** The Jan-2026 ClickHouse acquisition gives the steward an incentive to drive ClickHouse Cloud consumption (R5). If ops capacity is thin, Langfuse Cloud sidesteps the infra burden while keeping the OSS exit ramp — but you re-enter usage-based metering.
