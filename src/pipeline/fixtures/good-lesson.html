Why does a sine wave look identical after a 2π shift?

Most people first assume a wave "resets" because the clock ticks over — but the period is not a property of time, it is a property of the angle swept. That confusion is exactly what trips learners on phase. Let's pin down what is really repeating.

angle
The argument fed to sine, measured in radians — a full turn is 2π.
