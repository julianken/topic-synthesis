Phase shift of a sine wave

A common misconception is that the phase shift changes the shape of the wave — it does not. Adding a constant inside sine, sin(x + φ), slides the whole curve horizontally without altering its amplitude or period.

Predict

If we replace sin(x) with sin(x + π/2), does the peak move left or right? Think first, then read on.

Answer: a positive phase shifts the curve to the LEFT by π/2.
