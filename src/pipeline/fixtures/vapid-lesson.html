Sine waves

A sine wave is a smooth periodic oscillation. It is defined by the function sin(x). It has an amplitude, a period, a frequency, and a phase. The amplitude is the peak value. The period is the length of one cycle. The frequency is the number of cycles per unit. The phase is the horizontal shift. Sine waves are very important in mathematics and physics.
